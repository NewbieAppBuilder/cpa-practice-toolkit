# Client Document Checklist

**Important**: This checklist is derived from the firm's actual intake questionnaire. Review and adjust for each client's specific situation before sending. Tax law changes annually — verify that form names, thresholds, and credit eligibility reflect the current tax year.

After generating a checklist, ask: "Would you like me to save this as a Word document, copy-ready text, or paste-ready portal format?"

## How to Use This Skill

The checklist is built by combining:
1. **Universal items** — required for every client
2. **Profile-specific sections** — selected based on the client's filing type and intake answers
3. **Flagged items** — questions from the intake questionnaire that require a yes/no answer before the checklist is finalized

When generating a checklist, present it as a clean, client-ready document. Use plain language — spell out what each form is rather than citing only the form number.

## Universal Items (Every Client)

Include these on every checklist regardless of filing profile.

**Identity & Filing**
- Social Security cards or ITIN letters for taxpayer, spouse (if filing jointly), and all dependents being claimed
- Prior year federal and state tax returns — needed to verify AGI for e-filing and carry forward any unused credits or losses
- Bank account and routing number for direct deposit of any refund or direct debit of any balance due
- **Identity Protection PIN (IP PIN)**: If you, your spouse, or any dependent was issued an IP PIN by the IRS, provide all IP PINs — your federal return cannot be e-filed without them
- Filing status for prior year and any changes for the current year (marriage, divorce, separation, new child, death of spouse or dependent)

**Prior Year & Compliance**
- Copies of any IRS or state notices, letters, or audit correspondence received in the past 12 months — upload copies through the portal
- Documentation of any unpaid balances or installment agreements with the IRS or your state
- Copy of any federal or state extension filed last year, and confirmation that the return was ultimately filed

**Estimated Tax Payments**
- Records of any federal estimated tax payments (Form 1040-ES) made during the year — include the date and amount of each payment
- Records of any New York State or New York City estimated tax payments made during the year — include payment dates and amounts
- Any balance due paid with a prior year extension filing

---

## W-2 Employee Items

**Income**
- All W-2 forms from every employer during the tax year, including part-year jobs and second jobs
- Form W-2G for gambling winnings, if applicable

**Retirement & Benefits**
- Form 1099-R for any distributions from an IRA, 401(k), pension, or annuity taken during the year — note whether it was a normal distribution, early withdrawal, or required minimum distribution (RMD)
- Form 5498 if a Traditional IRA contribution was made (typically arrives in May — may require extension or amendment if not available at filing time)
- **HSA**: If enrolled in an HSA-compatible (high-deductible) health plan:
  - Form 1099-SA showing any HSA distributions taken during the year
  - Form 5498-SA showing contributions made to the HSA
  - Note whether contributions were made through payroll (already on W-2 Box 12 Code W) or made directly — direct contributions are deductible above the line

**Possible Itemized Deductions**
- Mortgage interest statement (Form 1098) from your lender
- Property tax bills showing the total amount paid during the year
- Charitable contribution receipts — all cash and non-cash donations; for non-cash donations over $500, a description and fair market value is required; for donations over $250, a written acknowledgment from the organization is required
- Student loan interest statement (Form 1098-E) — deductible even if not itemizing, up to $2,500 subject to income phase-out limits

## 1099 / Self-Employed Items

**Income**
- All Form 1099-NEC received (non-employee compensation from clients who paid $600 or more)
- All Form 1099-K received from payment processors (PayPal, Venmo Business, Cash App, Stripe, Square, etc.)
- Records of all cash, check, or ACH payments received that were NOT reported on a 1099 — all business income is taxable regardless of whether a 1099 was issued
- Invoices or a revenue summary if 1099s do not capture total income accurately

**Business Expenses**
Organize by category where possible. Keep all receipts. Commonly deductible items include:
- Advertising and marketing (website costs, ads, business cards, promotional materials)
- Software and subscriptions used for business (list each tool and monthly cost)
- Payments to subcontractors — provide the name, address, and SSN or EIN of anyone paid $600 or more; a Form 1099-NEC may need to be issued
- Home office — if you use a dedicated space regularly and exclusively for business: total home square footage, home office square footage, and either monthly rent or (if you own) mortgage interest, property taxes, insurance, and utilities paid during the year
- Business phone and internet — percentage used for business and monthly cost
- Vehicle and mileage — total business miles driven and total miles driven for the year; a contemporaneous mileage log is required; alternatively, actual vehicle expenses (gas, insurance, repairs, depreciation)
- Professional development — courses, books, and conferences directly related to maintaining or improving skills in your current profession
- Business meals — 50% deductible; must document the date, who was present, and the business purpose
- Business travel — airfare, hotel, and transportation for travel away from home for business; keep receipts and document business purpose
- Office supplies and equipment
- Business insurance (professional liability, general liability)
- Bank and merchant processing fees on business accounts
- Retirement contributions (SEP-IRA, Solo 401(k)) — contribution can be made up to the tax filing deadline including extensions; provide final contribution amount

**Self-Employment Health Insurance**
- Total health insurance premiums paid for yourself, your spouse, and your dependents during the year — deductible above the line if you are self-employed and not eligible for coverage through an employer plan

## Single-Member LLC Items

Include all 1099/Self-Employed items above, plus:

**Business Records**
- Profit & Loss statement or bookkeeping summary for the year (QuickBooks, Wave, spreadsheet, or bank statement export)
- Bank and credit card statements for all business accounts
- Clear separation of business and personal transactions — flag any mixed-use items

**Assets & Equipment**
- Any equipment, furniture, computers, or technology purchased during the year — provide the item description, cost, and date placed in service
- **Prior year depreciation schedules** — if this is not your first year as our client, provide the depreciation schedule from the prior year return; if we prepared the prior return, we have this on file and will note it accordingly
- Documentation of any business assets disposed of or sold during the year

**For LLCs with S-Corp Election (Form 1120-S)**
- Corporate bank statements and year-end balances
- Payroll records: W-2 issued to the owner-employee, copies of 941s filed, state payroll tax returns
- Total shareholder distributions taken during the year, separate from W-2 wages
- Officer health insurance premiums — must be included in Box 1 of the owner's W-2; provide the annual premium amount
- Basis tracking schedule if maintained (we will maintain this going forward; if switching from another preparer, provide prior year basis amount if available)
- Documentation of any loans between you and the LLC in either direction

## Investments & Assets

- Form 1099-DIV for dividend income from stocks, funds, or money market accounts
- Form 1099-INT for interest income from banks, savings accounts, and bonds
- Form 1099-B or Consolidated 1099 from your brokerage showing all investment sales during the year (stocks, mutual funds, ETFs, bonds)
- **Cryptocurrency and digital assets**: If you bought, sold, exchanged, or received any cryptocurrency or digital assets during the year — provide the complete transaction history from every exchange or wallet used (Coinbase, Kraken, Gemini, etc.); include purchases, sales, swaps, staking income, and any crypto received as payment
- K-1 forms from any partnerships, S-Corps, trusts, or estates in which you hold an interest — these often arrive in March or April; if you are expecting a K-1, notify us early so we can plan for an extension if needed
- Form 1099-S if you sold real property during the year
- Closing disclosure (CD or HUD-1) for any real estate sold — needed to calculate gain or loss and verify the sales price and transaction costs
- Records of original purchase price and capital improvements for any property sold

## Real Estate & Rental Property

- Form 1098 (mortgage interest statement) from your lender for your primary residence and any other property
- Property tax bills showing amounts paid during the year for all properties
- If you rented out property during the year: gross rents collected, and expenses for mortgage interest, property taxes, insurance, repairs and maintenance, management fees, and utilities; if the property is in its first year of rental, provide the purchase price and date of purchase for depreciation setup
- Airbnb or VRBO year-end income summary if applicable

## Health Coverage

- Confirmation of whether you had health insurance coverage for all 12 months of the year
- Form 1095-A if you obtained coverage through the Health Insurance Marketplace (Healthcare.gov or NY State of Health) — required to reconcile the Premium Tax Credit; your return cannot be finalized without it
- Form 1095-B or 1095-C if provided by your employer or insurance carrier (informational — not required to file, but useful for confirming coverage months)
- **HSA** (if applicable — see W-2 or Self-Employed sections above)

## Deductions & Credits

**Dependents**
- Full legal names, Social Security numbers, and dates of birth for all dependents being claimed
- For children of divorced or separated parents: documentation of custody arrangement and any Form 8332 (Release of Exemption) from the other parent if applicable

**Childcare & Dependent Care**
- Total childcare or dependent care expenses paid during the year
- Provider's name, address, and EIN or SSN — required for the Child and Dependent Care Credit

**Education**
- Form 1098-T from any college or university attended by you or a dependent — shows tuition paid and scholarships received
- Form 1098-E for student loan interest paid — deductible above the line up to $2,500, subject to income phase-out
- Records of other qualified education expenses (books, required supplies) if claiming the American Opportunity or Lifetime Learning Credit
- 529 plan distribution statements if distributions were taken during the year

**Medical Expenses**
- Total out-of-pocket medical, dental, and vision expenses paid during the year (only amounts exceeding 7.5% of AGI are deductible if itemizing)

**Charitable Contributions**
- Receipts for all cash donations — for any single donation of $250 or more, a written acknowledgment from the organization is required
- For non-cash donations: a description of items donated, the organization's name, date of donation, and your estimate of fair market value; for donations over $500, Form 8283 will be required

**Energy Credits**
- **Home energy improvements**: Documentation for any qualifying improvements made during the year — insulation, exterior windows and doors, HVAC systems (heat pumps, central air conditioning, furnaces, boilers), water heaters, electrical panel upgrades; provide receipts and manufacturer certification statements where available; the Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit (Form 5695) may apply
- **Residential clean energy**: Documentation for solar panels, solar water heaters, battery storage, or geothermal heat pumps installed during the year; provides a 30% credit with no dollar cap
- **Electric vehicle (EV)**: If you purchased a new or used plug-in electric vehicle during the year, provide the purchase contract, VIN, and dealer documentation; the Clean Vehicle Credit (new) or Previously Owned Clean Vehicle Credit (used) may apply; eligibility depends on vehicle MSRP, taxpayer income, and whether the dealer registered the sale with the IRS at time of purchase

**State and Local Taxes (SALT)**
- Total state and local income taxes paid (capped at $10,000 for itemizing purposes; note that New York residents subject to PTET may have additional deduction opportunities — discuss with your preparer)

## Retirement

- Contribution amounts and account types for any IRA, Roth IRA, SEP-IRA, or Solo 401(k) contributions made during the year or before the filing deadline
- Form 1099-R for any distributions from retirement accounts — confirm distribution type (normal, early, RMD)
- Form 5498 confirming IRA contributions (typically arrives in May)

## Foreign Accounts & Assets

- **FBAR (FinCEN 114)**: Did you have a financial interest in, or signature authority over, one or more foreign bank accounts or financial accounts at any point during the year where the aggregate value exceeded $10,000? If yes, FinCEN Form 114 must be filed separately through the BSA E-Filing System by April 15 (auto-extension to October 15 available). Failure to disclose carries significant civil and criminal penalties.
- **Form 8938 (FATCA)**: Did you hold specified foreign financial assets (foreign bank accounts, foreign stocks or securities, foreign partnership interests, etc.) with an aggregate value exceeding $50,000 at year-end (or $75,000 at any point during the year) for single filers? Thresholds are higher for MFJ filers and U.S. residents abroad. Form 8938 is filed with your federal return. Both FBAR and Form 8938 may be required simultaneously for the same accounts.
- Foreign income, pension, or retirement account distributions received during the year
- If you paid foreign taxes on foreign income, provide documentation — a foreign tax credit may be available

## Life Events (Flag These)

Ask about and flag any of the following that occurred during the tax year:
- Marriage or divorce — affects filing status, potential name change with SSA, and possible QDRO for retirement accounts
- Birth or adoption of a child — new dependent, Child Tax Credit, Child and Dependent Care Credit, potential Adoption Credit
- Death of a spouse or dependent — affects filing status; surviving spouse may qualify for Qualifying Surviving Spouse status for two years following the year of death
- Purchase of a first home
- Sale of a primary residence — Section 121 exclusion ($250,000 single / $500,000 MFJ) may apply; provide original purchase price, date of purchase, and records of capital improvements; must have owned and used as primary residence for 2 of last 5 years
- Receipt of an inheritance — generally not taxable income federally; inherited IRAs have distribution requirements; inherited real property receives a stepped-up basis
- Started or closed a business
- Moved to or from New York — part-year resident returns required for New York State and New York City; provide dates of domicile change

## Notes for the CPA

- **IP PIN**: Clients often forget they have an IP PIN. Ask explicitly and confirm all household members (spouse, dependents). A missing IP PIN will reject the e-filed return.
- **K-1 timing**: K-1s from partnerships and S-Corps often arrive in March or April, sometimes after the original filing deadline. Advise clients expecting K-1s early so an extension can be filed proactively.
- **1099-K threshold changes**: Remind clients that payment processors issue 1099-Ks at varying thresholds depending on year and platform. Clients may receive unexpected 1099-Ks; all business income is taxable regardless.
- **Crypto**: Many clients underreport crypto activity. Ask explicitly about all platforms used, including staking, DeFi, and NFT transactions.
- **Foreign accounts**: The foreign account question should be asked of every client, not just obvious international clients. Many clients have foreign accounts inherited or maintained in other countries without realizing the FBAR reporting threshold is low ($10,000 aggregate).
- **Prior-year notices**: Clients frequently forget to share IRS and state notices. Unaddressed notices can result in liens, levies, or missed response deadlines. Collect these at intake.
- **Estimated tax payments**: Clients often don't keep records. Ask for their online IRS account transcript or have them check IRS Direct Pay history if they don't have paper records.
- **EV credits**: Post-2022 EV credits are point-of-sale credits — if the dealer properly registered the transfer with the IRS, the credit flows through differently than prior-year EV credits. Confirm purchase year and whether the dealer registration was completed.
- **Depreciation schedules**: For LLC and self-employed clients not previously with the firm, the prior year depreciation schedule is essential for continuing to depreciate existing assets. Request it explicitly.

## Documentos Universales (Todos los Clientes)

**Identidad y Presentación**
- Tarjetas de Seguro Social o cartas de ITIN para el contribuyente, cónyuge (si presenta conjuntamente) y todos los dependientes que se van a reclamar
- Declaraciones de impuestos federales y estatales del año anterior — necesarias para verificar el AGI para la presentación electrónica y trasladar créditos o pérdidas no utilizados
- Número de cuenta bancaria y número de ruta para depósito directo de cualquier reembolso o débito directo de cualquier saldo a pagar
- **PIN de Protección de Identidad (IP PIN)**: Si usted, su cónyuge o cualquier dependiente recibió un IP PIN del IRS, proporcione todos los IP PINs — su declaración federal no puede presentarse electrónicamente sin ellos
- Estado civil del año anterior y cualquier cambio para el año actual (matrimonio, divorcio, separación, nuevo hijo, fallecimiento del cónyuge o dependiente)

**Año Anterior y Cumplimiento**
- Copias de cualquier aviso, carta o correspondencia de auditoría del IRS o del estado recibida en los últimos 12 meses — cargue las copias a través del portal
- Documentación de cualquier saldo pendiente o acuerdos de pago a plazos con el IRS o su estado
- Copia de cualquier prórroga federal o estatal presentada el año pasado, y confirmación de que la declaración fue finalmente presentada

**Pagos de Impuestos Estimados**
- Registros de cualquier pago de impuestos estimados federales (Formulario 1040-ES) realizados durante el año — incluya la fecha y el monto de cada pago
- Registros de cualquier pago de impuestos estimados del Estado de Nueva York o de la Ciudad de Nueva York realizados durante el año — incluya las fechas y montos de pago
- Cualquier saldo adeudado pagado con una presentación de prórroga del año anterior

---

## Documentos para Empleados con W-2

**Ingresos**
- Todos los formularios W-2 de cada empleador durante el año fiscal, incluyendo trabajos de tiempo parcial y trabajos secundarios
- Formulario W-2G por ganancias de juego, si corresponde

**Jubilación y Beneficios**
- Formulario 1099-R por cualquier distribución de una cuenta IRA, 401(k), pensión o anualidad tomada durante el año — indique si fue una distribución normal, retiro anticipado o distribución mínima requerida (RMD)
- Formulario 5498 si se realizó una contribución a una IRA Tradicional (normalmente llega en mayo)
- **HSA (Cuenta de Ahorros para la Salud)**: Si está inscrito en un plan de salud con deducible alto compatible con HSA:
  - Formulario 1099-SA que muestre cualquier distribución de HSA tomada durante el año
  - Formulario 5498-SA que muestre las contribuciones realizadas a la HSA
  - Indique si las contribuciones se realizaron a través de nómina (ya en el Casilla 12 Código W del W-2) o directamente — las contribuciones directas son deducibles

**Posibles Deducciones Detalladas**
- Estado de intereses hipotecarios (Formulario 1098) de su prestamista
- Facturas de impuestos sobre la propiedad que muestren el monto total pagado durante el año
- Recibos de donaciones caritativas — todas las donaciones en efectivo y en especie; para donaciones en especie de más de $500, se requiere una descripción y valor justo de mercado; para donaciones de más de $250, se requiere un reconocimiento escrito de la organización
- Estado de intereses de préstamos estudiantiles (Formulario 1098-E) — deducible hasta $2,500 sujeto a límites de reducción gradual de ingresos

## Documentos para Trabajadores por Cuenta Propia / 1099

**Ingresos**
- Todos los Formularios 1099-NEC recibidos (compensación de no empleados de clientes que pagaron $600 o más)
- Todos los Formularios 1099-K recibidos de procesadores de pago (PayPal, Venmo Business, Cash App, Stripe, Square, etc.)
- Registros de todos los pagos en efectivo, cheque o ACH recibidos que NO fueron reportados en un 1099 — todos los ingresos comerciales son gravables independientemente de si se emitió un 1099
- Facturas o un resumen de ingresos si los 1099 no capturan el total de ingresos con precisión

**Gastos del Negocio**
Organice por categoría donde sea posible. Guarde todos los recibos. Los artículos comúnmente deducibles incluyen:
- Publicidad y mercadeo (costos de sitio web, anuncios, tarjetas de presentación, materiales promocionales)
- Software y suscripciones usados para el negocio (liste cada herramienta y costo mensual)
- Pagos a subcontratistas — proporcione el nombre, dirección y SSN o EIN de cualquier persona a quien se le pagó $600 o más
- Oficina en el hogar — si usa un espacio dedicado regular y exclusivamente para el negocio: superficie total del hogar, superficie de la oficina en el hogar, y alquiler mensual o (si es propietario) intereses hipotecarios, impuestos sobre la propiedad, seguro y servicios públicos
- Teléfono y internet del negocio — porcentaje usado para el negocio y costo mensual
- Vehículo y kilometraje — millas de negocio conducidas en total y millas totales conducidas durante el año; se requiere un registro de kilometraje contemporáneo
- Desarrollo profesional — cursos, libros y conferencias directamente relacionados con el mantenimiento o mejora de habilidades en su profesión actual
- Comidas de negocios — 50% deducibles; debe documentar la fecha, quién estuvo presente y el propósito comercial
- Viajes de negocios — vuelos, hotel y transporte para viajes fuera del hogar por negocios
- Suministros de oficina y equipos
- Seguro del negocio (responsabilidad profesional, responsabilidad general)
- Tarifas bancarias y de procesamiento de pagos en cuentas comerciales
- Contribuciones de jubilación (SEP-IRA, Solo 401(k))

**Seguro de Salud para Trabajadores por Cuenta Propia**
- Total de primas de seguro de salud pagadas para usted, su cónyuge y sus dependientes durante el año

## Documentos para LLC de Socio Único

Incluya todos los documentos de Trabajadores por Cuenta Propia / 1099 anteriores, más:

**Registros del Negocio**

- Cronograma de seguimiento de base si se mantiene
- Documentación de cualquier préstamo entre usted y la LLC en cualquier dirección

- Formulario 1099-INT por ingresos de intereses de bancos, cuentas de ahorro y bonos

- **Criptomonedas y activos digitales**: Si compró, vendió, intercambió o recibió cualquier criptomoneda o activo digital durante el año — proporcione el historial completo de transacciones de cada exchange o billetera utilizada; incluya compras, ventas, intercambios, ingresos por staking y cualquier cripto recibido como pago
- Formularios K-1 de cualquier sociedad, corporación S, fideicomiso o herencia en la que tenga interés

## Deducciones y Créditos

**Dependientes**
- Nombres legales completos, números de Seguro Social y fechas de nacimiento de todos los dependientes que se van a reclamar
- Para hijos de padres divorciados o separados: documentación del acuerdo de custodia y cualquier Formulario 8332 del otro padre si corresponde

**Cuidado de Niños y Dependientes**
- Total de gastos de cuidado de niños o dependientes pagados durante el año
- Nombre, dirección y EIN o SSN del proveedor

**Educación**
- Formulario 1098-T de cualquier universidad o institución de educación superior asistida por usted o un dependiente
- Formulario 1098-E por intereses de préstamos estudiantiles pagados
- Registros de otros gastos educativos calificados (libros, suministros requeridos)
- Estados de cuenta de distribuciones del plan 529 si se tomaron distribuciones durante el año

**Gastos Médicos**
- Total de gastos médicos, dentales y de visión fuera de bolsillo pagados durante el año (solo los montos que excedan el 7.5% del AGI son deducibles si detalla)

**Donaciones Caritativas**
- Recibos de todas las donaciones en efectivo
- Para donaciones en especie: una descripción de los artículos donados, el nombre de la organización, la fecha de donación y su estimación del valor justo de mercado

**Créditos de Energía**
- **Mejoras de eficiencia energética en el hogar**: Documentación de cualquier mejora calificada realizada durante el año — aislamiento, ventanas y puertas exteriores, sistemas HVAC, calentadores de agua, actualizaciones del panel eléctrico; proporcione recibos y declaraciones de certificación del fabricante; puede aplicar el Crédito de Mejora de Eficiencia Energética del Hogar (Formulario 5695)
- **Energía limpia residencial**: Documentación de paneles solares, calentadores de agua solares, almacenamiento de baterías o bombas de calor geotérmicas instalados durante el año; proporciona un crédito del 30%
- **Vehículo eléctrico (EV)**: Si compró un vehículo eléctrico de enchufe nuevo o usado durante el año, proporcione el contrato de compra, VIN y documentación del concesionario; puede aplicar el Crédito por Vehículo Limpio

## Cuentas y Activos Extranjeros

- **FBAR (FinCEN 114)**: ¿Tuvo un interés financiero o autoridad de firma sobre una o más cuentas bancarias o financieras extranjeras en cualquier momento durante el año donde el valor agregado excedió $10,000? Si es así, el Formulario FinCEN 114 debe presentarse por separado antes del 15 de abril (prórroga automática hasta el 15 de octubre). El incumplimiento conlleva sanciones civiles y penales significativas.
- **Formulario 8938 (FATCA)**: ¿Tuvo activos financieros extranjeros especificados con un valor agregado que excedió $50,000 al final del año (o $75,000 en cualquier momento durante el año) para contribuyentes solteros? Ambos formularios FBAR y 8938 pueden ser requeridos simultáneamente para las mismas cuentas.
- Ingresos extranjeros, pensiones o distribuciones de cuentas de jubilación extranjeras recibidas durante el año
- Si pagó impuestos extranjeros sobre ingresos extranjeros, proporcione documentación — puede estar disponible un crédito fiscal extranjero

## Eventos de Vida (Marque Estos)

Pregunte y marque cualquiera de los siguientes que ocurrió durante el año fiscal:
- Matrimonio o divorcio
- Nacimiento o adopción de un hijo
- Fallecimiento de un cónyuge o dependiente
- Compra de una primera vivienda
- Venta de una residencia principal
- Recepción de una herencia
- Inicio o cierre de un negocio
- Mudanza hacia o desde Nueva York — se requieren declaraciones de residente de año parcial
