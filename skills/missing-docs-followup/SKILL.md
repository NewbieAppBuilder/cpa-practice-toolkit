# Missing Documents Follow-Up

**Important**: This skill generates client communication drafts for review by the CPA. Adjust the tone, urgency, and specifics to match your relationship with the client and your practice's communication style before sending.

Guidance for writing effective, professional follow-up emails to clients with outstanding tax documents.

## Communication Philosophy

Solo CPA client relationships are long-term. How you handle a nudge email matters. The goal is:
1. Get the documents
2. Preserve the relationship
3. Set clear expectations about consequences without being threatening

Most clients aren't ignoring you — they're busy, disorganized, or waiting on a document that hasn't arrived (a K-1, a corrected 1099, etc.). Assume good faith unless you have reason not to.

At the same time, you have a finite window to complete returns before deadlines, and your time is not free. The email should make the client feel supported, not scolded — while making the stakes clear.

## Tone Calibration by Urgency

Use the deadline provided to calibrate urgency:

**More than 3 weeks out:** Warm reminder. "Checking in to see if you've had a chance to track these down." No mention of extension yet.

**1–3 weeks out:** Friendly but clear. Name the deadline explicitly. Note that an extension may be needed if documents don't arrive in time. Do not be alarming.

**Less than 1 week out:** Direct and firm. State that an extension will be filed if documents are not received by [specific date]. Be matter-of-fact, not apologetic.

**Past the deadline (extension already filed or needed):** Inform the client of the extension, confirm no balance due is owed (or note that a payment is due), and reset expectations for the extended deadline.

## Email Structure

### Subject Line
Keep it specific and non-alarming. Options:
- "Quick Follow-Up — Your 2024 Tax Return"
- "A Few Items Still Needed for Your Return"
- "2024 Tax Prep — Outstanding Documents"

Avoid subject lines that sound like a collections notice ("URGENT: Action Required") unless the deadline is truly imminent.

### Opening
Acknowledge what the client has already provided. This confirms you received their materials and opens on a positive note. One sentence is enough: "Thanks for sending over your W-2s and mortgage statement — we're making good progress on your return."

If you have no information about what was already received, skip this line and open with the purpose of the email.

### The Ask
List outstanding items clearly and specifically. Use plain language — not tax form numbers alone.

Examples of plain language for common documents:
- Instead of "Form 1099-B" → "the year-end statement from your brokerage (Schwab, Fidelity, etc.) showing investment sales"
- Instead of "Form 8949 support" → "a list of any crypto transactions — purchases, sales, or swaps — made during the year"
- Instead of "Schedule K-1" → "the K-1 tax form from your partnership (or S-Corp, trust, etc.)"
- Instead of "Form 1095-A" → "your Health Insurance Marketplace (Healthcare.gov) year-end tax form — it shows the premium amounts and any subsidy received"
- Instead of "home office square footage" → "the total square footage of your home and the square footage of the room you use exclusively for work"

### Deadline and Consequence
State the deadline clearly. Frame the consequence matter-of-factly:
- "To complete your return by [date], I'll need these by [internal cutoff, typically 1–2 weeks before filing deadline]."
- "If I don't receive these by [date], I'll need to file a six-month extension to avoid any late-filing penalties. An extension is free and gives us until October 15 — it just delays when you'll receive any refund."
- If a balance may be due: "If you owe taxes, the payment would still be due by April 15 even with an extension — we can estimate the amount owed so you're not caught off guard."

### Call to Action
Make it easy to respond. Options:
- "You can reply to this email to send the documents, or upload them directly to [portal link]."
- "If you're still waiting on a document to arrive in the mail (like a K-1), just let me know and we can plan accordingly."
- "If you have questions about any of the items on this list, feel free to call or reply."

### Sign-Off
Standard professional close: "Best regards" or "Thank you" followed by name, title, phone, and email.

## Common Scenarios and Suggested Language

**Client is waiting on a K-1 that hasn't arrived yet**
"K-1s from partnerships and S-Corps often arrive in March or even early April. If yours hasn't arrived yet, you're not alone — just let me know when you receive it and I'll work it into your return. If it looks like it won't arrive before April 15, we'll file an extension."

**Client isn't sure if they have a 1099-K**
"Payment processors like PayPal, Venmo (business transactions), Stripe, and Square issue a Form 1099-K if you processed over a certain threshold. If you used any of these for business income, check your account's tax documents section. Even if you didn't receive a form, the income is still reportable."

**Client forgot about crypto**
"A quick reminder: cryptocurrency transactions — including buying, selling, trading, or receiving crypto as payment — are taxable events. If you had any crypto activity this year, please share the transaction history from each platform you used (Coinbase, Kraken, etc.)."

**Business client with incomplete expense records**
"If you're still pulling together your expense records, a bank or credit card statement works fine as a starting point. I can help you categorize from there — just send what you have and we'll work through it together."

---

## What Not to Do

- Do not say "per my previous email" — it reads as passive-aggressive
- Do not threaten late fees unless that's your actual policy and they've agreed to it
- Do not list 10+ items at once without context — group them and explain why each is needed
- Do not apologize for asking — requesting documents is your job
- Do not make the email so long that the client skims past the key items

# Español / Spanish Templates

## Correo de Seguimiento — Documentos Pendientes

### Líneas de Asunto (Subject Lines)

- "Seguimiento — Su Declaración de Impuestos 2024"
- "Algunos Documentos Aún Pendientes para Su Declaración"
- "Preparación de Impuestos 2024 — Documentos Pendientes"

Evite líneas de asunto que suenen como avisos de cobranza ("URGENTE: Acción Requerida") a menos que la fecha límite sea verdaderamente inminente.

### Apertura del Correo

Reconozca lo que el cliente ya ha proporcionado:
- "Gracias por enviar sus formularios W-2 y el estado de intereses hipotecarios — estamos avanzando bien en su declaración."
- "Le escribo para dar seguimiento a algunos documentos que aún necesitamos para completar su declaración de impuestos."

### La Solicitud — Lenguaje en Español para Documentos Comunes

En lugar de citar solo números de formulario, use lenguaje claro:
- En lugar de "Formulario 1099-B" → "el estado de fin de año de su corretaje (Schwab, Fidelity, etc.) que muestra las ventas de inversiones"
- En lugar de "Formulario K-1" → "el formulario fiscal K-1 de su sociedad (o corporación S, fideicomiso, etc.)"
- En lugar de "Formulario 1095-A" → "su formulario fiscal de fin de año del Mercado de Seguros de Salud (Healthcare.gov) — muestra los montos de prima y cualquier subsidio recibido"
- En lugar de "superficie de la oficina en el hogar" → "la superficie total de su hogar y la superficie de la habitación que usa exclusivamente para trabajar"

### Fecha Límite y Consecuencia

- "Para completar su declaración antes del [fecha], necesitaré estos documentos antes del [fecha interna de corte]."
- "Si no recibo estos documentos antes del [fecha], necesitaré presentar una prórroga de seis meses para evitar multas por presentación tardía. Una prórroga es gratuita y nos da hasta el 15 de octubre — solo retrasa cuándo recibirá su reembolso."
- Si puede haber un saldo a pagar: "Si usted debe impuestos, el pago seguirá siendo debido antes del 15 de abril incluso con una prórroga — podemos estimar el monto adeudado para que no se sorprenda."

### Llamada a la Acción

- "Puede responder a este correo para enviar los documentos, o cargarlos directamente a [enlace del portal]."
- "Si todavía está esperando que llegue un documento por correo postal (como un K-1), hágamelo saber y planificaremos en consecuencia."
- "Si tiene preguntas sobre cualquiera de los artículos en esta lista, no dude en llamar o responder."

### Despedida

Cierre profesional estándar: "Atentamente" o "Gracias" seguido del nombre, título, teléfono y correo electrónico.

## Escenarios Comunes — Lenguaje Sugerido en Español

**El cliente está esperando un K-1 que aún no ha llegado**
"Los formularios K-1 de sociedades y corporaciones S frecuentemente llegan en marzo o incluso a principios de abril. Si el suyo aún no ha llegado, no está solo — solo avíseme cuando lo reciba y lo incorporaré a su declaración. Si parece que no llegará antes del 15 de abril, presentaremos una prórroga."

**El cliente no sabe si tiene un 1099-K**
"Los procesadores de pago como PayPal, Venmo (transacciones comerciales), Stripe y Square emiten un Formulario 1099-K si procesó por encima de cierto umbral. Si usó alguno de estos para ingresos comerciales, revise la sección de documentos fiscales de su cuenta. Incluso si no recibió un formulario, los ingresos siguen siendo declarables."

**El cliente olvidó las criptomonedas**
"Un recordatorio: las transacciones de criptomonedas — incluyendo compra, venta, intercambio o recepción de criptomonedas como pago — son eventos gravables. Si tuvo alguna actividad de cripto este año, comparta el historial de transacciones de cada plataforma que utilizó (Coinbase, Kraken, etc.)."

**Cliente empresarial con registros de gastos incompletos**
"Si todavía está reuniendo sus registros de gastos, un estado de cuenta bancario o de tarjeta de crédito funciona bien como punto de partida. Puedo ayudarle a categorizar desde allí — solo envíe lo que tiene y trabajaremos juntos."
